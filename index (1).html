About me

The short version

I grew up in Moscow surrounded by people who cared a lot about how things looked and what they meant. Moved to Paris to study fashion, and somewhere along the way I realized I was less interested in making the clothes and more interested in making people pay attention to them.

That's what PR comes down to, I think. Finding the angle, building a story around it, getting it in front of the right people. I've been doing editorial shoots, putting together campaigns, and figuring out how to turn a designer's messy vision board into something an editor will actually open.

I'm still early in my career. But I work fast, I have good taste, and I always meet my deadlines.

How I work

It always starts with a mood board

Before I pitch anything or send a single email, I need to get the world right. This board, "Decadant Disarray," was for a collection about old-money opulence falling apart. Crumbling gilt, a chandelier that's been up too long, velvet worn thin at the elbows.

Sketches, swatches, and references live on the wall until they start making sense together. That's when I can figure out the press angle and what kind of images will actually stop someone mid-scroll.
